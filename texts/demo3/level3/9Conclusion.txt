The main expected benefit of the river restoration project on Gradaščica River, in all three segments is the increase of hydraulic capacity of the river and its stability. The project includes reshaping of the river cross-section profile to widen the channel. The widening of the profile is planned for all three segments,on a different scale, depending on the availability of the land and proximity of the urbanised structures. The segments S1 and S3 include construction of flood protection walls where existing structures (local roads, housing, etc.) do not allow extensive widening of the channel (on individual reaches of segments S1 and S3). In segment S2, the land availability allows higher increase in hydraulic capacity by channel widening and vegetated levees without incorporating grey protective measures. The channel bed is going to be stabilised by regulating the slope with a cascade of rock sills in combination with strategically placed root wads. Bank erosion is influenced by sheer stress decrease due to lower velocities of water flow (channel widening). Additional protection of banks is provided by rock riprap in combination with live staking and establishment of protective vegetation, and locally in segment S2 by vegetated cribwall. Detailed description of NbS to be implemented is provided in chapters “Potential NbS” and “Specification of the NbS to be demonstrated”.
