Building on the Level 2 assessment, the Level 3 analysis for the Gradaščica River focuses on highresolution identification of climate-related impacts. The primary hazards include fluvial flooding, bank erosion, and bed erosion, which threaten water infrastructure such as embankments, levees, and weirs. The relevant impacts include direct physical damage and increase in maintenance costs. Impact models are defined for each of the three segments of the Gradaščica River. The first segment, S1, extends for some 2.6 km and represents the upper part of the river within the Municipality of Ljubljana. In the course of segment S1, Gradaščica River passes under a highway (two times), and also under the highway.

Bokalce weir is situated in the S1 segment. It is characterised by higher urban confinement and proximity of housing. Consequently, the river is more regulated in that reach and has a high concentration of grey protective measures. Segment S2, the middle reach of the Gradaščica River inside Ljubljana, can be described as a more natural reach, with fewer regulating measures stretching for some 2 km. Segment S3, with the length of approximately 1.6 km, is the furthest downstream of the three defined segments. It reaches to the confluence of Gradaščica River with Ljubljanica River. This segment is again more confined by the urbanisation and presence of structures.
