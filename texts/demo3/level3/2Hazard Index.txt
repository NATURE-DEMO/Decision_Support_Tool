The calculation of the Hazard Index (HI) at Level 3 follows the same principles outlined in Level 2—combining projected variations in climate indicators relative to a baseline period and mapping them to threshold categories (e.g., LOW to EXTREME). The climate data used for Level 3 is the same as the one used in Level 2. The same data was used, as the data in Level 2 was already downscaled to the Municipality of Ljubljana. Further downscaling would exclude portions of the observed area. Downscaling of data to 10–50 km resolution would be applicable if, in Level 2, e.g. data for the country were used. This leads to the use of the same Hazard Index values as in Level 2, across all climate scenarios and time periods observed. Level 3 hazard characterization for segments S1–S3 of the Gradaščica River is in line with the Level 2 hazard classification. According to RCP 4.5, there is no variation for both observed time periods, as well as for mean values of climate indicators and upper limit of confidence intervals values. Here, we should mention again that this does not mean that there is no climate risk, but only that the existing risks and hazards will not worsen according to the RCP 4.5 climate scenario. According to RCP 8.5, for both considered time periods, higher variations are noted for acute hazards, referring to the extreme precipitation events. Short-term, heavy rainfall can lead to severe torrential flooding, triggering all the associated processes and hazards, potentially leading to bank and embankment failure, channel migration, etc. The highest variation (“HIGH” 3) is noted for 100-year return level of maximal daily precipitation for the long-term (2071–2100) RCP 8.5 climate scenario.
