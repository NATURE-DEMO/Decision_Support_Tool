Segment S1, the Zvolen Bypass Corridor (E77, 0.47 km), demonstrates the highest concentration of investment per unit length, with CAPEX of €5.80 million and OPEX of €0.58 million annually, reflecting the significant economic weight of a heavily trafficked dual carriageway road that carries around 15,000 vehicles per day. Segment S2, the Pustýhrad slopes (2.44 km), shows comparatively lower CAPEX (€3.25 million) and OPEX (€0.32 million), but generates higher revenues (€1.10 million) due to its cultural and tourism value, as well as its role in protecting heritage and utility assets such as a power line. Segment S3, the Neresnica section of I/66 (0.50 km), presents the highest overall investment level (€6.75 million) despite its short length, with OPEX of €0.68 million and modest revenues of €0.60 million, underlining the substantial economic implications of maintaining critical culverts and vegetated slopes exposed to pluvial flooding and landslide risks. The economic valuation across these segments confirms a diverse exposure profile: while S1 and S3 concentrate transport-related investment and operational value, S2 integrates heritage and utility assets with measurable economic returns, illustrating how different asset categories contribute to the overall economic risk landscape of the Zvolen infrastructure system.
