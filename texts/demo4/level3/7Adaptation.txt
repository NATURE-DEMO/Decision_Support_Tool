The final stage of the Level 3 risk assessment concerns the adaptation module, which builds on the hazard, exposure, vulnerability, and risk results to propose tailored solutions for each locality. The purpose of this stage is not only to reduce immediate risks but also to strengthen the long-term resilience, sustainability, and cost-effectiveness of critical infrastructure systems in Zvolen. The adaptation options were identified by combining grey infrastructure solutions, nature-based solutions (NbS), and governance/planning measures. Grey measures focus on engineering interventions such as drainage improvements, structural reinforcements, or resilient materials. NbS emphasize the stabilizing role of ecosystems (e.g., vegetation, forest management, bioengineering) in mitigating hazards such as landslides or floods. Governance and planning measures complement the technical solutions by addressing institutional readiness, operational procedures, and maintenance strategies. These options are aligned with the main hazards identified in the assessment — landslides, heavy precipitation, droughts, and heat waves — and tailored to the local characteristics of each segment. The adaptation options in Table below show that each locality requires a different balance of solutions. For S1 (Zvolen Bypass Corridor (E77)), the focus is on engineered drainage improvements and slope stabilization to reduce flood and landslide risk. For S2 (Pustý hrad slopes), the combination of NbS and grey measures is crucial, particularly to safeguard the heritage site, power infrastructure, and forested slopes from landslides and erosion. For S3 (Neresnica I/66 corridor), hybrid drainage and bioengineering interventions are prioritized to handle flash floods and slope erosion risks. Across all three localities, governance measures, such as updated maintenance regimes and the use of resilient construction materials, complement physical interventions and ensure sustainable outcomes.
