The calculation of the Exposure Index (EI) at Level 3 for Zvolen follows the same methodological principles as in Level 2, but with enhanced resolution and granularity. A detailed economic valuation of assets exposed to climate hazards has been performed for each locality, focusing on Capital Expenditures (CAPEX), Operational Expenditures (OPEX), and annual revenues. This step allows a more accurate estimation of the value at risk for each type of infrastructure and supports the assignment of Exposure Index scores using the thresholds. The Zvolen case study comprises three distinct localities — Zvolen Bypass Corridor (E77), Pustý hrad slopes, and Neresnica localities — each with unique asset portfolios and exposure profiles.
Segment S1, the Zvolen Bypass Corridor (E77), attains an Exposure Index of 4 (High) due to its considerable investment and maintenance requirements relative to modest revenues, reflecting the economic vulnerability of this heavily trafficked road to climate-related impacts. Segment S2, the Pustý hrad slopes, records a Medium Exposure Index (3), with lower CAPEX and OPEX but revenues linked to tourism and heritage use, indicating a moderate but contextually significant level of exposure. Segment S3, the Neresnica section of I/66, reaches the highest Exposure Index (5 – Very High), driven by the largest investment requirements among the three sites, combined with modest economic returns. These results emphasize the differentiated exposure profile of the Zvolen localities, with S1 and S3 representing transport-critical investments at high risk, and S2 reflecting heritage and utility exposure that, while economically smaller in scale, carries socio-cultural importance and potential cascading consequences.
