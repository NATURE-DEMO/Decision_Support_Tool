As in Level 2, the first step of the Level 3 analysis for Zvolen involves identifying climate-related impacts on infrastructure. For the three localities, this includes direct physical damage (e.g., slope destabilization at the E77 bypass, structural damage to heritage buildings at Pustý hrad, or culvert failure at Neresnica), operational disruption (e.g., traffic closures due to floods or landslides, service interruptions for tourism during wildfires), and increased maintenance costs (e.g., pavement deformation from heatwaves and drought-related cracking). The impact models are derived from Deliverable D.2.1 and have been adapted to the Zvolen context using Google Earth high-resolution imagery and terrain data. This allows the precise localisation of hazardprone assets (embankments, drainage channels, access roads, cultural monuments) and links them to specific hazard indicators (heavy rainfall days, drought probability, Fire Weather Index). In Level 3, the methodology supports advanced customization. The ad hoc impact models have been developed to capture site-specific dynamics:
• Zvolen Bypass Corridor (E77): Landslides – slope failure and cascading drainage blockage caused by concentrated rainfall (including combined impacts of freeze–thaw cycles) on pavements and embankments.
• Pustý hrad slopes: Wildfire propagation from forested slopes threatening cultural heritage and local settlements, as well as transmission lines; slope instability affecting both monuments and access roads.
• Neresnica locality: Combined flooding and debris flows in river valleys damaging culverts, disrupting transport, and degrading ecosystems.
