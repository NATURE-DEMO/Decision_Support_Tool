The Hazard Index (HI) at Level 3 for Globocica Dam demo site follows the same principles outlined in Level 2, combining projected variations in climate indicators relative to a baseline period and mapping them to threshold categories (e.g., LOW to EXTREME). The same climate datasets are used, with the methodology allowing for incorporation of national datasets and site-specific indicator calculations to refine hazard estimates where possible. For the Globocica Dam demo site, however, this refinement was not carried out, as the hazard predictions are already tailored to the location. Consistent with the Level 2 risk assessment, multiple climate scenarios (RCP4.5, RCP8.5) and time horizons (mid-term: 2050; long-term: 2100) have been considered to capture the trajectory of climate hazard intensification. Overall, the Level 3 hazard characterization for the Globocica Dam demo site reinforces Level 2 findings, with hydrometeorological hazards dominating the risk profile. Water- and solid mass-related hazards are the most critical under RCP8.5 for both time horizons.
• Water stress reaches MEDIUM to HIGH levels, marking it as a chronic risk.
• Extreme precipitation shows MEDIUM to HIGH scores across scenarios, emphasizing risks of spillway blockage and sedimentation.
• Mean annual temperature increases to VERY HIGH under RCP8.5 long-term, though with no direct impact on critical infrastructure.
• Cold waves and frost show no variation, implying negligible effects but warranting continued monitoring.
