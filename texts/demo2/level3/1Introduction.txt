The Level 3 Risk Assessment for the Bras, ov demonstration site represents the most detailed analytical stage of the multi-level climate risk evaluation framework applied within the NATURE-DEMO project. It builds on the Level 2 assessment that identified the main climate-related hazards affecting urban green spaces and further refines these findings through a spatially explicit and quantitative analysis. The principal goal of this stage is to assess the potential impacts of climatic changes on the performance, maintenance, and economic viability of Nature-based Solutions (NbS) implemented within Bras, ov’s urban green infrastructure. The analysis integrates local climate projections, site-specific exposure data, and vulnerability parameters, following the methodological principles of WP2. The approach also includes an economic component to quantify potential variations in operational and capital costs under changing climatic pressures, providing a sound basis for future investment prioritisation and policy guidance.

Methodological approach:
The Level 3 model integrates three analytical components: the Hazard Index (HI), the Exposure Index (EI), and the Vulnerability Index (VI), which together determine the Potential Risk Index (PRI). The Hazard Index quantifies the projected intensity and frequency of relevant climate hazards (temperature, drought, humidity, precipitation, and solar radiation) under the RCP4.5 and RCP8.5 scenarios for mid-term (2050) and long-term (2100) horizons.
