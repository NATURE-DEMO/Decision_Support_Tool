The results confirm that the principal risk drivers for Brasov’s green infrastructure are temperature and drought hazards, which directly influence vegetation health, irrigation requirements, and maintenance costs. Moderate risks are also associated with heavy rainfall events and localized erosion, though these are secondary compared to chronic heat and humidity stress. Despite these projected challenges, the overall risk remains moderate, reflecting the relatively robust adaptive capacity of the NbS system and its distributed design. The integration of drought-resistant vegetation, improved soil management, and systematic monitoring can maintain NbS functionality even under high-emission scenarios.
