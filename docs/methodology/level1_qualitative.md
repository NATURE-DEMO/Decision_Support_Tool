# Level 1 — Qualitative Risk Perception (RAMS SHEEP)

## Purpose

Level 1 provides a **qualitative, participatory risk perception** assessment based on the RAMS SHEEP framework. It establishes a shared understanding of infrastructure condition and vulnerability among stakeholders before any quantitative analysis, and benchmarks current baseline performance against four adaptation scenarios under natural hazard conditions.

This level is designed to be conducted in **workshop or interview settings** with infrastructure managers and operators.

---

## Key performance indicators

Five KPIs structure the assessment, covering the main dimensions of infrastructure performance:

| KPI | Dimensions |
|-----|-----------|
| **SRS** | Safety, Reliability, Security |
| **AM** | Availability, Maintainability |
| **EC** | Economy |
| **EV** | Environment |
| **HP** | Health / Politics |

Each KPI is scored on a **1–5 scale** (1 = excellent condition, 5 = critical / failing).

---

## Five assessment scenarios

Level 1 evaluates CI condition under five scenarios to capture the baseline and four adaptation configurations:

| Code | D2.1 label | Description |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **CI** | Baseline | Normal CI operation — no hazard event |
| **CI_H** | NH only | After hazard impact, no protective measures deployed |
| **CI_HG** | NH + GPI | After hazard with Grey Protective Infrastructure (dikes, levées, retaining walls) |
| **CI_HN** | NH + NbS | After hazard with Nature-Based Solutions |
| **CI_HNG** | NH + NbS + GPI | After hazard with combined NbS and grey infrastructure |

---

## Six-step process

1. **Define the CI asset** and its operational context
2. **Identify relevant natural hazards** for the location
3. **Score each KPI** for the baseline (NH only) scenario
4. **Re-score each KPI** for scenarios NH+GPI, NH+NbS, and NH+NbS+GPI
5. **Compute the overall condition index** per scenario (aggregated KPI score)
6. **Compare scenarios** to identify the most effective adaptation strategy

---

## Outputs

- Condition score per KPI per scenario (1–5 scale)
- Radar / spider chart comparing all five scenarios
- Qualitative narrative on NbS vs. GPI effectiveness
- Priority KPIs for deeper Level 2 analysis
